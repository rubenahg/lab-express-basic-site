About



Porsche AG, headquartered in Stuttgart, Germany, stands as one of the most iconic and prestigious automobile manufacturers in the world. Established by Ferdinand Porsche in 1931, the brand initially began as a design and engineering consultancy. It wasn't until 1948 that Porsche produced its first car, the Porsche 356, marking its foray into the world of automobile manufacturing.

Porsche's reputation in the automotive sector is unparalleled, intertwining luxury, performance, and innovation. The company's distinct commitment to engineering precision can be traced back to its founder, who was also instrumental in the design of the Volkswagen Beetleâ€”one of the most successful cars in history.

The Porsche 911, launched in the 1960s, has arguably become the brand's most iconic model, embodying the company's dedication to performance and design. Continuously evolving while maintaining its signature silhouette, the 911 has endeared itself to car enthusiasts around the globe for decades.

Beyond just producing vehicles, Porsche has a rich motorsport legacy. With countless victories in prestigious races such as the 24 Hours of Le Mans, Porsche's racing heritage complements its production line, often translating racetrack innovations into road cars.

In recent years, Porsche has diversified its product range to include SUVs like the Cayenne and Macan, and even ventured into the electric vehicle market with the Taycan. This adaptability and commitment to future technologies underscore the brand's forward-thinking approach, ensuring it remains at the forefront of the automotive industry.

As a part of the Volkswagen Group since 2012, Porsche continues to drive its legacy forward, merging tradition with innovation, and shaping the future of performance motoring.
